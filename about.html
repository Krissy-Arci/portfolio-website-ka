About

My Story

I’ve always been a creative person who loves to draw, paint, analyze, organize and beautify. I started taking art lessons at a young age, which eventually lead me to PAFA where I earned my Bachelor of Fine Arts degree. After college, I started teaching at a Preschool and as a substitute instructor for the Mural Arts Program. I loved teaching and the students who I was so lucky to spend time with, but eventually, the cycle of putting so much energy into my classroom and curriculum, then having to start over with a whole new group of students every few months started to take an emotional and physical toll. I began nannying because I wanted the opportunity to form a more meaningful and lasting bond with my clients, and to see my efforts as a childcare professional come to fruition as I helped the children I cared for meet their developmental goals, and grow into their unique, intelligent and vibrant selves. After years of juggling teaching and childcare with maintaining a work-life balance, I felt that I was missing something, and that I had strayed away from any creative practice. I did some soul-searching to figure out what I really wanted from my life and career. I looked into going back to school to study various subjects that interested me, like Interior Design or Biology. Ultimately, after much research and many conversations with designers and others in the tech field, I found that User Interface Design was the perfect way for me to combine the things I care about most in my career- creativity, independence, and most importantly, helping people! I could manage a classroom, create and implement lesson plans successfully, think on my feet during unexpected roadblocks (there are a lot of those when working with children!), exercise empathy, work on a team, and build strong client relationships, so I already had a valuable set of transferable soft skills, but I needed to learn how to use the tools to become a UI Designer. So I saved up, bought a new MacBook and enrolled in a CareerFoundry bootcamp. After over 600 hours of project-based UI/UX Design and over 100 hours studying CSS and coding with HTML and JavaScript, I now have a strong set of UI/UX and Frontend Development skills that I’m ready to use in the field. Through my incredibly supportive professional network and collaborative efforts, I’ve also gained valuable insight from other designers and web developers. My only regret is not making the career pivot into UI Design sooner! I live with my husband and our two hilariously mischievous cats, Martha and Smudge (aka my office assistants). When I’m not in my home office/ artist studio designing apps or working on pet portrait commissions, you can find me in my tiny kitchen, listening to jazz and preparing an unnecessarily elaborate snack.
